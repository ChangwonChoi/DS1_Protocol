Arrows

DS1: Protocol

  • “Arrows” / "Arrows"
    • 의미 없는 요소로 포스터를 보는 사람을 늘려보자. / Let's try to increase the number of people looking at the posters by adding meaningless elements.
    • N25 건물 2층 휴게공간 앞 포스터가 붙어있는 공간이 있다... / There is a space with posters in front of the lounge on the 2nd floor of the N25 building. Some people do not even realize that posters are there, and even if they do, few look at them closely. I want to subtly add small, indirect elements to increase the number of people paying attention to these posters.
    • 프로토콜: / Protocol: Arrow post-its are placed on each poster, pointing to a specific poster.
    • 변화시키고 싶은 것: / What to reveal: I wanted to make the intention of guiding people's attention invisible.
    • 왜 화살표인가? / Why this addition: The arrows don’t have any special meaning, but people look at the poster in the direction of the arrow, wondering if there is a hidden meaning.
    • 예상 효과: / Expected impact: With just a few arrows, people get curious, pay attention, and naturally read the posters in a certain direction.
  • What: / Place sticky notes with arrows pointing to specific posters
  • Where: / On each poster in front of the 2nd-floor resting area
  • When: / 6/2–6/5 no sticky notes, 6/8–6/10 with sticky notes, 9 AM–12 AM
  • How: / Long-term observation using a time-lapse camera
  • Need: / Unused phone with tripod, sticky notes with arrows
  • Problem: / Permission to put sticky notes on posters, privacy/portrait rights
  • Beauty: / Only sticky notes are added (no visible elements like QR codes), and the intention is subtle—sparking pure curiosity.
  • 화살표 전 / Before arrows:
    • 6/4 (Thu): 1 person (12 PM – 9 PM)
    • 6/5 (Fri): 2 people (10 AM – 9 PM)
    • 6/6 (Sat): 0 people (10 AM – 5 PM)
  • 화살표 후 / After arrows:
    • 6/8 (Mon): 3 people (9 AM – 9 PM)
    • 6/9 (Tue): 0 people (9 AM – 9 PM)
    • 6/10 (Wed): 14 people (9 AM – 9 PM)
  • 관찰 내용: / Observations:
    • 6/6: Very few people looked at the posters; mostly waiting by water dispenser/microwave.
    • Camera move not possible due to charging, tripod visibility, gaze not visible, filming already exposed. (Video not public due to privacy concerns.)
    • 6/8: Removed filming notice post-it; more people looked at posters, but camera drew more attention. Arrows not very noticeable; need bigger/brighter post-its. Camera doesn’t capture all gaze.
    • 6/9: Camera behind sofa less effective; replaced post-its with brighter/larger ones. No one stopped to look. Camera moved back and arrows bolder. (Video not public due to privacy concerns.)
    • 6/10: Camera back to original position; arrows bolder; extra post-it added. Huge success: 14 people read them; many noticed changes and discussed. After snacks, attention shifted away. (Video not public due to privacy concerns.)

직접적인 문구나 특별한 장치 없이 화살표 포스트잇을 붙인 것만으로 포스터를 보는 사람들이 늘어났다. / Simply by placing arrow Post-it notes without any direct message or special device, more people started looking at the posters.

처음에는 의도와 예상 반응과 다른 부분이 있었다. 계속 관찰하고 조정한 끝에 마지막 날 많은 반응을 이끌어냈다. / At first, there were some differences between intended and expected reactions. Observed people's reactions and continuously made adjustments, and on the last day, many responses were elicited.

명확한 의도가 없었기에 사람들의 호기심을 자극했고, 사람들이 서로 이야기하는 모습을 볼 수 있었다. / No clear or direct intention made curiosity arise, and people even discussed what it was, achieving the goal of making them reflect on the arrows.

타임랩스에는 소리 기록이 없어 어떤 반응이 오갔는지는 모르지만 매우 궁금하다. / Since the timelapse did not record sound, we do not know what reactions occurred among people, which is very curious.
