Arrows

DS1: Protocol

“Arrows”
Let's try to increase the number of people looking at the posters by adding meaningless elements.

There is a space with posters in front of the lounge on the 2nd floor of the N25 building. Some people do not even realize that posters are there, and even if they do, few look at them closely. I want to subtly add small, indirect elements to increase the number of people paying attention to these posters.

Protocol: Arrow post-its are placed on each poster, pointing to a specific poster.

What to reveal: I wanted to make the intention of guiding people's attention invisible.

Why this addition: The arrows don’t have any special meaning, but people look at the poster in the direction of the arrow, wondering if there is a hidden meaning.

Expected impact: With just a few arrows, people get curious, pay attention, and naturally read the posters in a certain direction.

What: Place sticky notes with arrows pointing to specific posters.

Where: On each poster in front of the 2nd-floor resting area.

When: 6/2–6/5 no sticky notes, 6/8–6/10 with sticky notes, 9 AM–12 AM.

How: Long-term observation using a time-lapse camera.

Need: Unused phone with tripod, sticky notes with arrows.

Problem: Permission to put sticky notes on posters, privacy/portrait rights.

Beauty: Only sticky notes are added (no visible elements like QR codes), and the intention is subtle—sparking pure curiosity.

Before arrows:

  • 6/4 (Thu): 1 person (12 PM – 9 PM)
  • 6/5 (Fri): 2 people (10 AM – 9 PM)
  • 6/6 (Sat): 0 people (10 AM – 5 PM)

After arrows:

  • 6/8 (Mon): 3 people (9 AM – 9 PM)
  • 6/9 (Tue): 0 people (9 AM – 9 PM)
  • 6/10 (Wed): 14 people (9 AM – 9 PM)

6/6: As expected, very few people normally looked at the posters, mostly waiting by the water dispenser or using the microwave.

Tried to move the camera to a less visible location, but couldn’t due to hard charging, tripod visibility, gaze movement not visible, and filming already exposed. (The video is not made public due to privacy concerns.)

6/8: Removed the post-it indicating filming, more people looked at posters, but people were more interested in the camera. Arrows were not very noticeable; need brighter and bigger post-its. Camera does not capture all gaze.

6/9: Camera moved behind sofa but was less effective, replaced post-its with brighter colors, no one stopped to look. Camera returned to original position and arrows made bolder. (Video not public due to privacy concerns.)

6/10: Camera in original position, arrows bolder, extra post-it to indicate something. Huge success: 14 people including professor read them. Many noticed poster changes, some discussed. After snacks were set up by CA, fewer people looked at posters. (Video not public due to privacy concerns.)

Simply by placing arrow Post-it notes without any direct message or special device, more people started looking at the posters.

At first, there were some differences between intended and expected reactions. Observed people's reactions and continuously adjusted, and on the last day, many responses were elicited. No clear or direct intention made curiosity arise, and people even discussed what it was, achieving the goal of making them reflect on the arrows. Since the timelapse did not record sound, we do not know what reactions occurred among people, which is very curious.
